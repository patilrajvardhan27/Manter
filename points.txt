WEB RESEARCH INSIGHTS
(Niche points from psychology research, safety apps, and dating industry data — to inform app features)

GREEN FLAG PSYCHOLOGY (Research-Backed)
24) He can disagree respectfully, take responsibility for his mistakes, apologize sincerely, and repair after arguments — relationship researchers consistently identify conflict repair as the #1 predictor of long-term relationship success (2022 study)
25) He ensures your voice genuinely influences his decisions — a 2020 study found that when both partners feel their voice truly matters, relationship quality stays high and emotional security deepens over years
26) He has a secure attachment style — he is consistent, responsive, and emotionally available (based on Bowlby & Ainsworth attachment theory; secure attachment is the foundation of healthy long-term relationships)
27) He can admit when he is wrong — people who admit mistakes are rated as more competent and more likable than those who refuse to (2022 study); this directly ties to emotional maturity (point 9)

BEHAVIORAL RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR (What the App Should Screen Against)
28) Uses controlling language — "you should wear this", "I decide where we go", "I don't like your friends"
29) Shows anger escalation patterns in text — starts calm, becomes aggressive when questioned or told no
30) Guilt-trips after a "no" — makes her feel bad for saying no to anything physical, social, or personal
31) Rushes physical or emotional intimacy — creates a sense of urgency or pressure before trust is established
32) Dismisses her feelings — "you're overreacting", "you're too sensitive", "why do you always make it a big deal"
33) Shows jealousy or possessiveness early — questions who she is talking to, wants to track her location, gets upset when she spends time with others
34) Makes fun of women to fit in with his friend group (social misogyny) — laughs at sexist jokes, stays silent when others disrespect women

SAFETY & VERIFICATION INSIGHTS (From Tea App, Red Flag App, Cray App)
35) ID verification is the single most effective tool against fake profiles and catfishing — apps that require it see dramatically less harassment
36) Community-sourced warnings work — the Tea app (women warn each other about men in their city) went #1 on the App Store in 2025 purely because women needed a place to share real experiences with real names and faces
37) AI chat analysis catches manipulation before it escalates — Red Flag app's AI analyzes screenshots for gaslighting, love-bombing, and concerning patterns and alerts women before they're in too deep
38) Pattern detection saves women who miss early signs — Cray app (founded after its creator survived a traumatic betrayal) found users consistently miss early behavioral warning signs; explanatory content showing why a behavior matters is more effective than just flagging it
39) Timed date check-in saves lives — a simple timer that alerts emergency contacts if the woman does not confirm she is safe is one of the highest-rated safety features in women's dating app reviews

APP DESIGN PRINCIPLES (From Bumble, Hinge, and Dating Industry Research)
40) Women-first design — letting women control who can message them reduces harassment and measurably improves mental health outcomes for women on the platform (Bumble model, validated over 10 years)
41) Quality over quantity in matching — Hinge's prompt-based profiles (not just photos) produce the highest date-to-match ratio of any major dating app; selective women respond better to fewer, better-matched candidates than endless swiping
42) Ambition and stability verification builds trust — exclusive apps like Luxy that require income verification attract more serious, commitment-oriented users on both sides
43) Curated, ranked candidate pools outperform endless swipe feeds for selective users — a woman who knows why she is being shown someone (compatibility score, quality match) is far more likely to invest in the connection than a random swipe result
