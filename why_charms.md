Every major dating app — Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, Badoo — has the same flaw: they surface men based on photos and proximity. Women with high standards are stuck swiping through endless profiles with no meaningful signal about whether a man is emotionally mature, respectful, or genuinely kind.

The result: women who've been hurt, gaslit, controlled, or disrespected have no choice but to repeat the same trial-and-error process every time they open a dating app. They want evidence before they invest time and emotional energy. Current apps give them none.

**Charms solves this.** It is the first app that turns "is he actually a good man?" from a gut feeling into a verified, data-backed score.

## 4. The 23-Quality Framework — Turning Feelings Into Data

The 23 qualities in `points.txt` are not arbitrary — they map directly to what relationship psychology research identifies as the strongest predictors of long-term relationship success:

**Emotional maturity** (qualities 9, 14, 18):
> A 2022 study found that partners who can admit when they're wrong, apologize sincerely, and repair after conflict are rated as significantly more competent and trustworthy. Conflict repair is one of the strongest predictors of relationship longevity.

**Mutual influence** (qualities 1, 2, 3, 6):
> A 2020 study by relationship researchers found that when both partners feel their voice genuinely matters, relationship quality stays high over years. A man who respects a woman's decisions and supports her reaching heights directly predicts this.

**Secure attachment** (qualities 4, 7, 8, 12):
> Bowlby and Ainsworth's attachment theory (foundational to modern relationship psychology) shows that a partner who is consistent, responsive, and emotionally available (qualities 8, 12, 20) creates the secure base that allows both people to thrive.

**Practical partnership** (qualities 19, 20):
> Research on relationship satisfaction consistently shows that equal division of household labor is one of the strongest predictors of long-term relationship quality — particularly for women in heterosexual relationships.

Charms converts these research-backed qualities into a weighted score. Women set their priority weights. The app surfaces men who match their specific priorities — not a generic "good man" template.

## 5. The Competitive Moat — Network Effects

Charms's community rating system gets more valuable with every user who joins.

**The flywheel:**
1. Women join → they rate men they've dated on 23 qualities
2. More ratings → more accurate community scores for every man on the platform
3. Better scores → women make better decisions → better dates → women trust the app more
4. Women trust the app → more women join → flywheel accelerates

This is the same network effect that made Yelp and Airbnb reviews powerful. Unlike photo-based apps, Charms's value is not in the photos — it's in the data. And data compounds.

**The flip side:** Men who behave well get discovered more. Men who behave poorly get poor community scores and fewer matches. This creates a direct incentive for men to be the kind of partner women are looking for. **The app actively shapes behavior on the platform.**

## 6. Safety as a Competitive Advantage

Women don't trust dating apps. They have been harassed, stalked, catfished, and assaulted through them. Charms treats safety not as a checkbox feature but as the core product:

- **ID verification**: Real name, real face. Fake profiles get caught.
- **Behavioral quiz**: Scenario-based questions are hard to fake because there's no obviously "correct" answer — patterns emerge.
- **AI red flag detection**: Claude AI scans conversations for manipulation, controlling language, gaslighting, and boundary violations before they escalate.
- **Community warnings**: Women who've dated a man can leave anonymous ratings. The Tea app went #1 on the App Store in 2025 purely for this feature — Charms builds it in natively.
- **Date check-in**: Timed safety check-in before first dates alerts emergency contacts if the woman doesn't confirm she's safe.
- **Screenshot blocking in chat**: Private conversations stay private.
- **Biometric app lock**: The app locks with Face ID/fingerprint when not in use.

Each safety feature increases women's trust. More trust = more women. More women = better community data. Better data = better matches. Safety is not a cost — it is the product's competitive advantage.
